A simple weekly routine can help keep hijabs fresh and beautiful longer. Many women begin by separating scarves by fabric type before washing. Delicate materials often need gentler treatment than everyday fabrics. Organized care prevents unnecessary damage over time. Small habits create long-term benefits.

Wash frequently used hijabs regularly to maintain freshness. Use mild detergent and appropriate water temperature for the material. Many women air dry scarves to preserve shape and color. Folding items neatly after drying also reduces wrinkles. Proper care keeps fabrics looking polished.

It is also useful to inspect pins, magnets, and undercaps weekly. Replace damaged accessories before they cause problems. Clean storage spaces can help protect scarves from dust. Rotating frequently used hijabs may reduce wear. Maintenance supports a tidy collection.
