Deciding to wear the hijab is a personal journey that doesn’t always receive immediate support from everyone around you. Sometimes, even close friends or family may not fully understand your decision. This can feel discouraging, especially in the beginning.

However, it’s important to remember that your journey is yours alone. You are not responsible for meeting everyone’s expectations, but rather for staying true to your own values and beliefs.

Over time, people may begin to understand and respect your choice, even if they initially questioned it. Consistency and confidence often speak louder than words.

It’s also helpful to find a supportive community, whether online or offline. Being surrounded by people who share similar values can make your journey feel less lonely and more empowering.
