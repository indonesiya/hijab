Proper care is essential to maintain the quality and longevity of hijabs. Many women invest in a variety of fabrics, from delicate chiffon to durable cotton, and each requires specific handling. Washing, drying, and storing hijabs correctly can prevent damage and fading. Understanding fabric care ensures that hijabs remain fresh and beautiful over time. Taking a little extra care can make a big difference in their durability.

Washing hijabs with mild detergent and cold water is highly recommended. Harsh chemicals and hot water can weaken the fabric and cause colors to fade. Hand washing is often the safest method, especially for delicate materials. If using a washing machine, placing hijabs in a laundry bag can provide extra protection. Gentle handling helps preserve the texture and quality of the fabric.

Drying methods also play a crucial role in maintaining hijab condition. Air drying is the best option as it prevents shrinkage and fabric damage. Direct sunlight should be avoided for certain materials, as it can cause discoloration. Ironing should be done at the appropriate temperature based on the fabric type. Using a steamer can be a safer alternative for delicate hijabs.
