Beginning the hijab journey is a meaningful step, but like any new experience, it comes with its own set of challenges and learning curves. Many women make common mistakes at the start, not because they lack understanding, but because they are still adjusting to a new lifestyle and mindset.

One common mistake is trying to achieve perfection too quickly. The journey is not about immediate transformation, but about gradual growth and consistency. Putting too much pressure on yourself can lead to frustration and burnout.

Another mistake is comparing yourself to others. Social media often shows the best moments, making it easy to feel inadequate. However, every journey is unique, and progress should be measured based on personal growth rather than external standards.

Lack of preparation can also create unnecessary difficulties. Not having the right clothing, understanding styling basics, or managing daily routines can make the transition feel overwhelming. Taking small, practical steps can make a big difference.
